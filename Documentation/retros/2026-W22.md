Direct sessions +21.9% WoW (below the +50% surge threshold) and engagement only -2.8% — the bot-filter rule does **not** trigger. But Direct's ~7% engagement means crawler/drive-by share is high in both windows. Real-human signal is returning users: 616 this week vs 691 prior (-10.9%), at 66% engagement and ~6.4-min sessions. So real traffic moved *down* ~11% while the headline shows +20%. Treat new-user counts as bot-noisy; MRR, paid conversions, and returning-user behaviour are the load-bearing numbers.

## The single biggest gap

**New paid conversions collapsed to ~3/wk and have stayed there for a month — and we cannot see why, because the upload → preview → download → paid path is uninstrumented.**

Three 1-star `/upload` reports cluster 05-16 → 05-20 (a settings/login loop, a broken image pipeline producing wrong text, and `&nbsp` artifacts in card links). They are real, but the conversion cliff began the week of **05-04**, two weeks earlier — so the regressions deepened a hole that was already open rather than causing it. The common root cause is **shipping velocity with zero funnel visibility**: 100+ changelog entries landed on or around the core convert path from 05-12 → 05-30 while conversions were already falling. That is exactly how a login loop and a destabilised image pipeline ship unnoticed. No instrumentation entry exists anywhere in the changelog; `Documentation/specs/` is empty.

This maps to **both** halves of the CLAUDE.md goal: a wobbling convert path is not "simplest/fastest," and a conversion engine stuck at ~3/wk with no visibility cannot scale toward 300K.

## The one decision

**Freeze new surfaces for one week. Make the core convert path trustworthy and visible — nothing else.**

1. **Instrument upload → preview → download → paid** with durable server-side events (the `events` table, not GA4) so the next conversion cliff is visible same-day. Load-bearing: we have flown blind for four weeks.
2. **Fix the three confirmed `/upload` regressions** as the first thing the new instrumentation watches.

No new landing pages, no chat iteration, no SEO this week. The April → May data is the argument: the highest shipping volume of any month coincided with an 85% fall in new conversions. More surface is not the move. No conversion-count target is committed until instrumentation reveals the true baseline — committing to a number we cannot see is how we got here.

Spec to follow (one bundled PR, draft in flight): durable upload-funnel instrumentation + the three `/upload` regression fixes — server events first, then the fixes those events watch.
